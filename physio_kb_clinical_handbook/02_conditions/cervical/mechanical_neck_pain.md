# Mechanical / Non-Specific Neck Pain

## Diagnosis
- neck-dominant pain where symptoms are modified by posture, movement, or load
- no dominant pattern for fracture, myelopathy, inflammatory disease, infection, malignancy, or acute nerve-root compression
- diagnosis is clinical; routine imaging is not required in uncomplicated cases

## Clinical classification (use for treatment matching)
- **mobility-deficit dominant:** pain/stiffness with rotation/extension, local restriction
- **movement-control / endurance dominant:** low tolerance to sustained positions and repeated tasks
- **headache-associated neck pain:** neck impairment coexisting with cervicogenic headache features
- **mixed profile:** common; prioritize irritability + function over rigid subtype labels

## Differential diagnosis and red flags
Differentiate from:
- cervical radiculopathy (dermatomal arm pain/paresthesia, myotomal weakness, reflex change)
- cervicogenic headache vs migraine / primary headache
- shoulder-referred pain
- thoracic outlet pattern, peripheral nerve entrapment

Urgent referral triggers:
- major trauma, suspected fracture/dislocation
- progressive bilateral neuro signs, gait change, hand clumsiness, bowel/bladder change (possible myelopathy)
- unexplained weight loss, fever, severe night pain, cancer/infection risk profile
- non-mechanical severe pain that is rapidly worsening

## Subjective exam priorities
- mechanism: trauma vs insidious onset
- pain map (neck only vs arm symptoms) and headache behavior
- irritability profile: 24-hour pattern, sleep disruption, flare triggers
- sustained-load exposures: desk, driving, caregiving, lifting
- neuro warning symptoms (weakness, numbness, dexterity decline)
- beliefs/avoidance and work-impact priorities

## Objective exam priorities
- baseline comparable signs: active ROM, quadrant loading, symptom response
- repeated-movement or sustained-position testing for dose guidance
- upper-quarter neurological screen when arm symptoms are present
- deep neck flexor/extensor endurance and scapulothoracic contribution
- thoracic mobility screen where extension/rotation limits load tolerance
- avoid over-weighting palpation tenderness alone

## Useful tests and limits
- **Spurling, distraction, ULTT, cervical rotation ROM:** useful as a cluster for radiculopathy probability shift; not stand-alone diagnosis
- **Craniocervical flexion test:** helpful for endurance/motor-control targeting; not a pain generator test
- **Cervical flexion-rotation test (if headache):** supports C1–2 dysfunction pattern; cannot rule out primary headache disorders

## Management (conservative first-line)
- clear explanation, reassurance, and activity continuation
- symptom-calming movement exposure (not prolonged rest)
- progressive neck + scapulothoracic loading with dosage based on irritability
- short-term manual therapy only as an adjunct to active care
- ergonomic modification only for clearly relevant sustained-load tasks

## Exercise progression logic
1. **Calm-irritable:** frequent ROM, low-load isometrics/endurance, movement snacks.
2. **Capacity rebuild:** progress endurance and strength through functional ranges.
3. **Return-to-demand:** repeated rotation/extension tolerance, carries/pulls/presses, task-specific conditioning.
Progress when post-session flare remains acceptable and settles by next day.

## Escalation / imaging / procedures
- imaging if trauma, progressive neuro deficit, myelopathy suspicion, systemic red flags, or failure with persistent diagnostic uncertainty
- consider medical co-management for medication support in severe acute irritability
- injection/surgery rarely indicated for uncomplicated mechanical neck pain
