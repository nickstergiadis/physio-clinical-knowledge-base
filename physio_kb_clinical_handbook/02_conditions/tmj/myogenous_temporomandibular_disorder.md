# Myogenous Temporomandibular Disorder

## Diagnosis
- jaw/facial pain primarily from masticatory muscle loading
- pain provoked by chewing, prolonged talking, clenching, or parafunctional habits
- no dominant intra-articular locking pathology

## Differential diagnosis and referral triggers
Differentiate from disc displacement, dental pathology, trigeminal neuralgia, primary headache disorders, and referred cervical pain.

Refer when red flags appear: unexplained swelling, infection signs, trauma/fracture suspicion, cranial nerve deficits, severe unexplained weight loss, or persistent progressive limitation.

## Subjective exam priorities
- pain behavior with chewing/speaking/clenching
- morning jaw symptoms and sleep bruxism clues
- stress, sleep quality, and parafunctional behaviors
- headache/neck symptom overlap

## Objective exam priorities
- mandibular opening pattern and range
- symptom reproduction with resisted/prolonged jaw loading
- palpation of masseter/temporalis for familiar pain
- cervical contribution screen

## Useful tests and limits
- palpation and resisted jaw tests help phenotype muscle irritability but are examiner dependent
- ROM measures track change but do not define pathology alone

## Management and progression
- education: symptom pacing, parafunction reduction, sleep/stress strategies
- gentle jaw mobility and relaxation drills in high irritability phase
- progressive jaw endurance/control work and graded chewing exposure
- include cervical/upper-quarter loading when co-contributors are present

## Escalation / imaging / procedures
- imaging not routine for straightforward myogenous TMD
- escalate to dentistry/orofacial pain if non-response, major occlusal concerns, or atypical findings
