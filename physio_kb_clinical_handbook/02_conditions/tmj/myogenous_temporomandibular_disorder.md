# Myogenous Temporomandibular Disorder

## Clinical summary
Myogenous TMD is a muscle-dominant temporomandibular disorder involving pain and sensitivity in the masticatory system, often associated with clenching, bruxism, stress-related overactivity, and cervical contribution.

## Typical presentation
- jaw, cheek, temple, or preauricular pain
- pain with chewing, talking, yawning, or clenching
- morning tightness / fatigue
- headache and neck pain may coexist
- mouth opening may be painful rather than mechanically blocked

## Subjective findings to prioritize
- pain location and whether it feels muscular, joint-line, or diffuse
- chewing tolerance, tough foods, gum chewing, talking duration
- bruxism / clenching, stress, sleep quality
- headache, neck pain, posture, desk work
- previous splint / dental / physio response

## Objective findings
- tenderness and symptom reproduction in masticatory muscles
- painful but not necessarily severely blocked opening
- clench / bite provocation
- cervical contribution often present
- symptom change with relaxation, pacing, and movement correction may be informative

## Conservative management
- education and reassurance
- habit awareness: clenching, gum chewing, wide-yawn overload
- supervised jaw exercise / stretching when appropriate
- postural and cervical exercise where relevant
- manual trigger point / soft tissue techniques as adjuncts
- consider dental / splint collaboration when indicated

## Exercise priorities
- jaw relaxation and resting position awareness
- controlled opening / closing without overloading
- gentle mobility only within acceptable irritability
- postural and cervical support work
- chewing exposure graded back up

## When to refer / reconsider
- major locking or acute dislocation features
- marked trismus, infection, or fracture suspicion
- primary dental pain suspicion
- persistent severe pain with high psychosocial complexity needing multidisciplinary input

## Patient education points
- Clicking is not always the main problem; pain and function matter more.
- Many chronic jaw pain cases respond better to conservative, reversible care than to aggressive procedures.
- Daytime clenching awareness can be as important as formal exercise.
