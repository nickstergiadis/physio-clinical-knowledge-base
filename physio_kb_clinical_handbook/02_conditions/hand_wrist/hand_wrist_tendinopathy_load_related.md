# Hand / Wrist Tendinopathy (Load-Related)

## Diagnosis
- local tendon-region pain linked to repetitive gripping, lifting, typing, tools, or gym load
- pain reproduced with resisted loading and/or stretch of involved tendon
- diagnosis is clinical; identify dominant tendon structure and aggravating task

## Clinical classification
- **reactive/high irritability:** pain spikes with light load, morning stiffness, low load tolerance
- **capacity-deficit/lower irritability:** tolerates daily use but fails under volume/intensity demands
- **mixed tendon + neural/mechanical irritability:** common in complex hand use jobs

## Differential diagnosis and red flags
Differentiate from:
- de Quervain tenosynovitis
- intersection syndrome
- CMC/MCP arthropathy
- TFCC pathology, carpal instability
- peripheral nerve entrapment (median/ulnar/radial)

Referral/red flags:
- suspected tendon rupture, acute loss of active function
- fracture/dislocation suspicion or persistent post-traumatic pain
- progressive neurologic deficit
- inflammatory or infectious signs (heat, swelling, fever, multiple joints)

## Subjective exam priorities
- exact task exposures (volume, grip type, tool ergonomics)
- symptom behavior during/after load and next-day response
- work/sport deadlines and non-negotiable loads
- previous tendon episodes and response to splint/injection/rest

## Objective exam priorities
- palpation and resisted testing to localize symptomatic tendon
- grip/pinch strength baseline and side-to-side comparison
- active/passive motion limits and symptom reproduction pattern
- neural screen if paresthesia/weakness is reported
- functional task simulation (typing, tool grip, lift/carry)

## Useful tests and limits
- resisted tendon-specific tests: useful for load targeting but not pathology grading
- provocative tendon compression/friction tests: can over-irritate, interpret cautiously
- dynamometry: objective for progression; pain can mask true capacity early

## Management
- short-term load calming without complete rest
- progressive tendon and grip loading matched to irritability
- selective splinting/taping for temporary task support
- workstation or tool modifications when clearly exposure-driven

## Exercise progression logic
1. isometric and low-range isotonic loading in tolerated dosage
2. progressive isotonic/eccentric-concentric loading with grip progression
3. velocity, endurance, and task-specific load exposure (work/sport simulation)
4. maintain exposure dosing to prevent rapid re-spike

## Escalation / imaging / procedures
- ultrasound/MRI only when rupture, atypical pathology, or failed management with diagnostic uncertainty
- injection may be considered for selected tenosynovitis patterns; avoid repeated passive-only pathways
- surgical referral is uncommon and usually reserved for structural failure or persistent severe disability
