# Hand / Wrist Tendinopathy (Load-Related)

## Clinical summary
This page covers non–de Quervain load-related hand and wrist tendinopathy presentations where pain is linked to repetitive gripping, keyboard / mouse use, tools, gym exposure, or post-traumatic overload. Evidence is thinner here than for larger tendons, so treatment should be pragmatic and symptom-guided.

## Typical presentation
- local tendon-region pain
- pain with gripping, lifting, typing, racquet / gym tasks, or repetitive wrist motion
- tenderness over involved tendon
- resisted loading usually reproduces symptoms
- stiffness may be mild; capacity loss is often more obvious

## Management principles
- identify the dominant provocative exposure
- calm load enough to reduce irritability
- avoid prolonged complete rest
- preserve motion and hand use
- progressively reload tendon and grip capacity
- use splinting selectively, not as indefinite dependence

## Clinical caution
- evidence for exercise therapy in hand / wrist tendinopathy is limited
- be careful with overconfident protocol claims
- progression should be individualized and based on irritability, task demands, and function
