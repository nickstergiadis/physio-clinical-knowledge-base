# Cervical Clinical Reasoning

## Main buckets
- mechanical neck pain
- neck pain with mobility deficit
- neck pain with movement-coordination / endurance deficit
- cervicogenic headache pattern
- neck pain with radicular features or upper-quarter neural contribution
- post-traumatic and whiplash-related patterns

## High-value discriminators
- headache features and relationship to neck movement
- arm symptoms below the shoulder / hand
- irritability and sleep disturbance
- trauma history
- red-flag and vascular questions when indicated

## Practical management logic
- do not diagnose from posture alone
- pair local findings with functional tolerance and psychosocial context
- use exercise and exposure as core care; use manual therapy as an adjunct
- escalate screening if symptoms are non-mechanical, progressive, or neurologically changing
