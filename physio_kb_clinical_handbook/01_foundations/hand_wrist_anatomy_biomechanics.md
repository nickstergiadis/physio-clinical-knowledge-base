# Hand / Wrist Anatomy + Biomechanics

## Core anatomy
- wrist motion combines radiocarpal and midcarpal contributions
- thumb function depends heavily on CMC mobility plus coordinated tendon loading
- radial wrist pain often involves first dorsal compartment structures in De Quervain-type patterns
- grip and pinch tasks transmit force through the wrist, thumb, and forearm as one system

## Practical biomechanics
- repetition alone is rarely the full explanation; capacity, task setup, pacing, and irritability matter
- radial deviation with thumb loading can strongly provoke first dorsal compartment symptoms
- gripping, typing, lifting, and childcare tasks often combine wrist position plus sustained thumb demand
