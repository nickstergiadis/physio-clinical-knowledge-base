# Hip Clinical Reasoning

## Main diagnostic buckets
- intra-articular / joint-dominant: hip OA, FAIS, labral or chondral contribution
- lateral hip: GTPS / gluteal tendinopathy-bursopathy spectrum
- musculotendinous / load-related: hip flexor, adductor, proximal hamstring, gluteal overload
- referred pain: lumbar spine, SIJ-adjacent, abdominal / pelvic sources when clinically indicated
- post-op: arthroscopy, gluteal repair, arthroplasty

## Useful discriminators
### OA-leaning
- age / stiffness / reduced IR and flexion
- painful and stiff ADL pattern
- loading and gait tolerance reduced
- night pain can occur but should still fit an MSK pattern

### FAIS-leaning
- younger / active patient
- flexion-adduction-internal rotation sensitivity
- groin pain with deep flexion, pivoting, sitting, or squatting
- ROM loss often present but variable

### GTPS-leaning
- lateral hip pain
- side-lying intolerance
- pain with walking, stairs, or single-leg tasks
- tendon compression and abductor load sensitivity dominate

## Screen beyond the hip when needed
- lumbar spine if symptoms are buttock-dominant, neurologic, or not reproduced clearly at the hip
- abdominal / pelvic red flags if symptoms are non-mechanical
- fracture / stress injury concerns if pain is high-irritability and weight-bearing intolerance is disproportionate

## Practical management logic
- identify dominant pain source and irritability
- match load to tissue tolerance and function
- use education plus progressive loading as the backbone
- use manual therapy and symptom modifiers as adjuncts, not the long-term plan
