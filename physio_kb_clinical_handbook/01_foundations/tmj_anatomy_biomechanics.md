# TMJ Anatomy + Biomechanics

## Core anatomy
- temporomandibular joint includes mandibular condyle, temporal fossa, and intra-articular disc
- mandibular motion combines rotation and translation
- masseter, temporalis, medial and lateral pterygoids are key force contributors
- cervical posture, upper-quarter tension, sleep bruxism, and psychosocial load can interact with jaw symptoms

## Practical biomechanics
- opening, protrusion, and lateral deviation should be considered as movement patterns, not isolated structures
- disc and mobility complaints are often movement-sensitive
- myogenous presentations are often load, clench, stress, and endurance sensitive
