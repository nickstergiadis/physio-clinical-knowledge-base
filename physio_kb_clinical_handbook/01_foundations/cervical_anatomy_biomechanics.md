# Cervical Anatomy + Biomechanics

## Core anatomy
- upper cervical region contributes substantially to flexion-extension and rotation coupling relevant to headache and upper-neck symptoms
- lower cervical segments contribute to combined flexion-extension, side-bending, and rotation
- deep cervical flexors and extensors support segmental control
- scapulothoracic and thoracic mechanics often influence cervical loading tolerance

## Practical biomechanics
- sustained low-load postures can provoke symptoms without implying tissue damage
- repeated or loaded extension / rotation may be symptom-sensitive in some presentations
- upper-quarter neural and vascular structures may contribute to arm symptoms and must be screened when indicated
- jaw, thoracic, and shoulder girdle factors can perpetuate neck-dominant pain

## Common patterns
- local neck pain / stiffness
- neck pain with scapular / upper trapezius referral
- neck pain with headache
- neck pain with arm symptoms requiring neuro screen
