# Elbow / Forearm Anatomy + Biomechanics

## Core anatomy
- humeroulnar and humeroradial joints manage flexion-extension load transfer
- proximal radioulnar joint drives pronation-supination
- common extensor and common flexor tendon origins are frequent lateral / medial pain generators
- radial, median, and ulnar nerves can mimic or coexist with tendon pain

## Practical biomechanics
- gripping increases load across the elbow and forearm
- wrist extension / supination demands often aggravate lateral elbow pain
- wrist flexion / pronation demands often aggravate medial elbow pain
- cervical and neural contributions should be screened when symptoms are diffuse, burning, numb, or weak
