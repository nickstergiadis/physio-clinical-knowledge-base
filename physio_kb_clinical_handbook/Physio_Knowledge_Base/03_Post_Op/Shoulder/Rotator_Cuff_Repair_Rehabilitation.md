## 1. Scope / procedure
Post-op care following arthroscopic or open rotator cuff repair.

## 2. Important surgeon- or protocol-dependent cautions
Procedure-specific restrictions vary by surgeon/protocol.

## 3. Healing and rehab phases
Protective -> progressive loading -> performance integration.

## 4. Early priorities
Pain/effusion control, safe mobility, early ROM targets, quadriceps/rotator cuff activation as relevant.

## 5. ROM/loading precautions
Respect graft/tissue healing windows and surgeon protocol.

## 6. Strength and function progression
Build bilateral strength, movement quality, and task-specific capacity before higher-demand return.

## 7. Common complications / escalation triggers
Persistent effusion, ROM plateau, instability episodes, wound concerns, DVT/red-flag symptoms.

## 8. Return-to-activity considerations
Use criterion-based progression with symptom and function markers.

## 9. Outcome measures
KOOS-JR/HOOS-JR/SPADI/PSFS as region appropriate.

## 10. Evidence / protocol notes
Evidence update needed.

## 11. Practical clinic pearls
Coordinate early with surgeon team and document protocol deviations clearly.
