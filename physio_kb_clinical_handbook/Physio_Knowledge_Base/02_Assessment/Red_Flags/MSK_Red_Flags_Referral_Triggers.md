## 1. Purpose
Structured regional assessment to classify presentation, identify differential pivots, and set first-line management.

## 2. Key questions
- Primary symptom behavior, irritability, 24-hour pattern.
- Mechanism/onset and aggravating/easing factors.
- Functional restrictions and patient-priority goals.

## 3. Observation
Posture, movement confidence, compensations, swelling or asymmetry as relevant.

## 4. Active testing
Regional AROM quality and symptom response; repeated movement where useful.

## 5. Passive testing
PROM/end-feel and symptom reproduction where clinically indicated.

## 6. Resisted testing
Capacity and pain response in key muscle groups.

## 7. Neuro screen where relevant
Targeted myotome/dermatome/reflex/neural mechanosensitivity screen based on symptom distribution.

## 8. Special tests
Use as hypothesis-supporting tools only; combine with history and physical exam cluster.

## 9. Differential pivots
Identify findings that shift likelihood toward alternate diagnosis or non-MSK cause.

## 10. Comparable signs
Track 1-3 repeatable signs (pain-provoking functional task, ROM marker, or load test).

## 11. Red flags
Escalate if red flag criteria present or evolving.

## 12. Documentation examples
- "Pain with __, eased by __, irritability __/10."
- "Comparable sign: __ changed from __ to __ post-session."
- "Plan: load progression + education + review interval."
