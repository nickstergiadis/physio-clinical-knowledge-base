## 1. Clinical summary
High-incidence injury with meaningful recurrence risk. Early protected loading plus progressive neuromuscular rehab is key.

## 2. Key presentation patterns
Lateral ankle pain/swelling after inversion injury, reduced weight-bearing tolerance, instability sensation.

## 3. Differential diagnosis
Fracture, syndesmotic sprain, osteochondral lesion, peroneal tendon injury.

## 4. Red flags / referral triggers
Ottawa rule positive region, gross instability/dislocation signs, neurovascular compromise.

## 5. Subjective exam priorities
Mechanism, immediate swelling/function, prior sprains, sport/work demands.

## 6. Objective exam priorities
Ottawa rules screen, edema/bruising map, ROM, ligament palpation, balance/proprioception.

## 7. Useful special tests and their limits
Anterior drawer/talar tilt assist grading but acute pain/swelling can reduce accuracy.

## 8. Common impairments / comparable signs
Dorsiflexion loss, peroneal weakness, sensorimotor deficits, fear with cutting/landing.

## 9. Management principles
PEACE & LOVE style principles, early movement, brace/tape when needed, progressive strength-balance-power rehab.

## 10. Exercise progression logic
Phase from pain/swelling control -> ROM/strength -> dynamic balance/plyometric -> return to sport tasks.

## 11. Patient education priorities
Recurrence prevention requires ongoing balance and calf/peroneal conditioning.

## 12. Outcome measures
FAAM, PSFS, NPRS, single-leg balance tests.

## 13. Escalation / imaging / injection / surgical considerations
Escalate for suspected syndesmosis, fracture, persistent instability, or failure to progress.

## 14. Evidence update notes
Evidence update needed: verify return-to-sport criteria standards by sport level.

## 15. Practical clinic pearls
Do not discharge after pain settles; complete neuromuscular phase to reduce recurrence.
