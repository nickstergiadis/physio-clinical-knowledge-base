## 1. Clinical summary
Knee OA is a chronic joint condition with variable pain and function impact. Exercise and weight/load management are core care pillars.

## 2. Key presentation patterns
Activity-related knee pain, morning stiffness usually short duration, load intolerance in squatting/stairs/walking.

## 3. Differential diagnosis
Inflammatory arthropathy, meniscal tear with mechanical locking, referred hip/spine pain, fracture.

## 4. Red flags / referral triggers
Hot swollen joint with systemic features, unexplained night pain, trauma with inability to weight-bear.

## 5. Subjective exam priorities
Load tolerance, activity pattern, flare profile, weight/metabolic factors, patient goals.

## 6. Objective exam priorities
Gait, ROM, effusion, quadriceps strength, sit-to-stand tolerance, step control.

## 7. Useful special tests and their limits
No single special test confirms OA; cluster clinical features with imaging only when clinically necessary.

## 8. Common impairments / comparable signs
Quadriceps weakness, reduced confidence with loading, low aerobic capacity.

## 9. Management principles
Exercise therapy, education, self-management, and load progression remain backbone. Adjuncts (manual therapy/taping) are short-term aids.

## 10. Exercise progression logic
Progress from pain-limited strengthening to functional capacity blocks (stairs, sit-to-stand, walking volume).

## 11. Patient education priorities
Expected symptom variability, pacing, flare management, role of long-term strength and aerobic work.

## 12. Outcome measures
KOOS-JR or KOOS, LEFS, PSFS, NPRS, 5xSTS.

## 13. Escalation / imaging / injection / surgical considerations
Consider imaging when diagnosis uncertain, severe symptom progression, or pre-procedural planning. Discuss injections/surgical referral for persistent high disability despite quality rehab.

## 14. Evidence update notes
Evidence update needed: confirm current recommendations on injection sequencing and prehab pathways.

## 15. Practical clinic pearls
Dose exercise to tolerable symptoms rather than pain elimination; consistency beats intensity spikes.
