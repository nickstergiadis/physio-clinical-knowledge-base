## 1. Clinical summary
Common overuse-related anterior knee pain syndrome, often load-capacity mismatch across patellofemoral joint tasks.

## 2. Key presentation patterns
Retropatellar/peripatellar pain with stairs, squatting, running, prolonged sitting.

## 3. Differential diagnosis
Patellar tendinopathy, meniscal pathology, fat pad irritation, hip referral.

## 4. Red flags / referral triggers
True locking, major effusion post-trauma, inability to weight-bear after injury.

## 5. Subjective exam priorities
Training/load change, symptom irritability, footwear/surface change, psychosocial context.

## 6. Objective exam priorities
Single-leg squat quality, step-down tolerance, hip/knee strength, patellofemoral provocation tasks.

## 7. Useful special tests and their limits
Patellar compression/provocation tests support pattern but are not definitive in isolation.

## 8. Common impairments / comparable signs
Hip abductor/extensor weakness, quadriceps endurance deficit, movement strategy under load.

## 9. Management principles
Primary treatment is progressive exercise plus load modification. Taping/orthoses can be short-term adjuncts.

## 10. Exercise progression logic
Pain-calibrated squat/lunge and hip strengthening -> running/cutting return progression when symptoms stable.

## 11. Patient education priorities
Recovery is often gradual; relapse risk drops with sustained strength and load management.

## 12. Outcome measures
AKPS optional, LEFS, PSFS, NPRS.

## 13. Escalation / imaging / injection / surgical considerations
Imaging usually unnecessary unless atypical symptoms, trauma, or failed care with diagnostic uncertainty.

## 14. Evidence update notes
Evidence update needed: population-specific guidance for adolescent athletes.

## 15. Practical clinic pearls
Treat the person-task context; avoid overemphasis on static alignment findings.
