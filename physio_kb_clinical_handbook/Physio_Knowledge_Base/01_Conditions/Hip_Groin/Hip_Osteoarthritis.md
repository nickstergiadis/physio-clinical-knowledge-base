## 1. Clinical summary
Starter condition page for clinic use. Prioritize symptom pattern, risk profile, and function-based progression while awaiting full evidence pass.

## 2. Key presentation patterns
- Common pattern cluster described in assessment and condition-specific load profile.
- Confirm irritability and functional impact early.

## 3. Differential diagnosis
Use regional differential framework and screen for alternate pain generators.

## 4. Red flags / referral triggers
Escalate for serious pathology signs, progressive neurologic deficit, infection, fracture, or vascular concern.

## 5. Subjective exam priorities
Mechanism, onset, irritability, prior episodes, comorbidity, work/sport demands, patient goals.

## 6. Objective exam priorities
Regional ROM, strength, function tasks, neuro screen where relevant, comparable sign tracking.

## 7. Useful special tests and their limits
Use clusters, not single-test diagnosis. Interpret in context of full history/exam.

## 8. Common impairments / comparable signs
Typical impairments include load intolerance, movement control deficits, and deconditioning.

## 9. Management principles
Exercise-based management is primary. Passive interventions are adjunctive and short-term only.

## 10. Exercise progression logic
Progress from symptom-calibrated loading to capacity and task-specific return.

## 11. Patient education priorities
Set expectations for time course, flare plan, and self-management role.

## 12. Outcome measures
Select at least one region PROM + PSFS + pain scale.

## 13. Escalation / imaging / injection / surgical considerations
Use imaging/referral when diagnosis uncertain, red flags present, or failure to progress despite appropriate care.

## 14. Evidence update notes
Evidence update needed.

## 15. Practical clinic pearls
Clinical pragmatism note: prioritize adherence and function over perfect test-normalization.
