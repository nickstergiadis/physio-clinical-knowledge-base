## 1. Clinical summary
Broad clinical syndrome including subacromial pain presentations. Diagnosis is pattern-based, not single-test dependent.

## 2. Key presentation patterns
Painful arc, night discomfort, overhead load intolerance, weakness/pain on cuff loading.

## 3. Differential diagnosis
Adhesive capsulitis, cervical referral, glenohumeral OA, instability, full-thickness tear.

## 4. Red flags / referral triggers
Acute traumatic loss of active elevation, infection signs, unexplained severe night pain/systemic signs.

## 5. Subjective exam priorities
Irritability, overhead demands, trauma history, sleep disruption, occupational/sport loads.

## 6. Objective exam priorities
AROM/PROM comparison, resisted cuff testing, scapular control, cervical screen.

## 7. Useful special tests and their limits
Hawkins-Kennedy/Neer/painful arc can support likelihood but have limited standalone accuracy.

## 8. Common impairments / comparable signs
Cuff endurance deficits, scapulothoracic dyskinesis, load-management mismatch.

## 9. Management principles
Progressive loading and function-first programming; passive care is adjunctive and time-limited.

## 10. Exercise progression logic
Isometrics/isotonics in tolerated range -> elevation and external rotation endurance -> task/sport-specific loads.

## 11. Patient education priorities
Pain during loading can be acceptable within monitored limits; avoid prolonged unloading.

## 12. Outcome measures
SPADI, QuickDASH, NPRS, PSFS.

## 13. Escalation / imaging / injection / surgical considerations
Ultrasound/MRI when traumatic tear is suspected, persistent marked weakness, or failed conservative care with surgical consideration.

## 14. Evidence update notes
Evidence update needed: refine guidance on injection timing and cuff tear subgrouping.

## 15. Practical clinic pearls
Focus on restoring confidence in overhead movement, not only isolated cuff strength.
