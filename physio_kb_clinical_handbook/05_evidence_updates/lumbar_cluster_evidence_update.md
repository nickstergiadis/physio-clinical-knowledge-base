# Lumbar Cluster — Evidence Update

## Core guideline anchors
### NICE NG59 (updated 2020)
Key points:
- consider risk stratification such as STarT Back
- do not routinely image in non-specialist settings
- provide tailored advice and support for self-management
- consider exercise programmes for episodes / flare-ups
- manual therapy only as part of a package including exercise, with or without psychological therapy
- do not offer traction, acupuncture, routine electrotherapies, or belts / corsets
- for sciatica, do not offer gabapentinoids, other antiepileptics, oral corticosteroids, or benzodiazepines
- do not offer opioids for chronic sciatica
- consider epidural local anaesthetic plus steroid for acute and severe sciatica
- consider spinal decompression when non-surgical care has failed and imaging matches sciatic symptoms

Source:
- https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng59/chapter/recommendations

### WHO chronic primary low back pain guideline (2023)
Key points:
- recommends a holistic, person-centred, biopsychosocial approach
- recommends education/self-care support, exercise, some physical therapies, psychological therapies such as CBT, and NSAIDs where appropriate
- advises against routine use of lumbar braces/supports, traction, and opioids for most people in most contexts

Sources:
- https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240081789
- https://www.who.int/news/item/07-12-2023-who-releases-guidelines-on-chronic-low-back-pain

### VA/DoD low back pain CPG (2022)
Key points:
- recommends imaging/labs when serious or progressive neurological deficits or red flags are present
- recommends against routine imaging for acute axial low back pain without focal neurological deficits or red flags
- suggests mental health screening as part of low back pain evaluation
- suggests clinician-directed exercise programmes for low back pain
- suggests CBT for chronic low back pain
- suggests spinal mobilization/manipulation for chronic low back pain
- finds insufficient evidence for or against mechanical lumbar traction and for or against gabapentin/pregabalin
- suggests NSAIDs
- suggests against opioids for chronic low back pain and against systemic corticosteroids and benzodiazepines

Source:
- https://www.healthquality.va.gov/guidelines/Pain/lbp/VADODLBPCPGFinal508.pdf

### JOSPT / AOPT low back pain CPG revision (2021)
Key points:
- supports exercise across acute and chronic low back pain, with more specific recommendations by subgroup/presentation
- supports trunk muscle strengthening/endurance, multimodal exercise, aerobic exercise, aquatic exercise, and general exercise for chronic low back pain
- supports specific trunk activation and movement control for selected chronic LBP subgroups and for some patients with leg pain
- supports pain neuroscience education as an adjunct alongside other PT interventions rather than as a stand-alone strategy

Source:
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10508241/

## Practical synthesis for this KB
Use this hierarchy:
1. triage and red flags first
2. no routine imaging in straightforward non-specialist presentations
3. active management is the default
4. exercise is a core intervention across most lumbar presentations
5. manual therapy is adjunctive, not primary
6. psychological-informed care matters when recovery barriers are present
7. be conservative with medication expectations, especially for sciatica and chronic pain
8. escalate for progressive neurological deficit, severe refractory sciatica, or diagnostic uncertainty
