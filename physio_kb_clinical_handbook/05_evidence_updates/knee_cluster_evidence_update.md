## Practical takeaways that changed or were reinforced
### Knee OA
- Diagnose clinically in typical presentations; routine imaging is not required.
- Exercise is core treatment.
- Weight management matters when relevant.
- Topical NSAIDs are first-line medication support for knee OA.
- Manual therapy is not a stand-alone treatment.
- Hyaluronan injections are not recommended in NICE guidance.
- OA management should stay active and function-centered.

### Patellofemoral pain
- Exercise therapy plus education is primary care.
- Knee-targeted exercise can be paired with hip-targeted exercise.
- Adjuncts such as prefabricated foot orthoses, taping, movement/running retraining, or manual therapy are selected to match the individual presentation rather than applied routinely to everyone.
- Surgery does not have a routine role in typical PFP.

### Degenerative meniscal tear
- Exercise-based care remains first-line for most degenerative meniscal presentations.
- Arthroscopic surgery does not show superior long-term pain/function outcomes over conservative treatment in most middle-aged degenerative cases.
- Careful selection still matters for true locked knee, displaced tears, or persistent disabling symptoms after a solid conservative trial.
