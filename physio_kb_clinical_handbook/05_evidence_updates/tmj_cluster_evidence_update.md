# TMJ / Orofacial Cluster Evidence Update

## Scope of this update
This page tracks higher-value evidence for the current TMJ / orofacial cluster:
- myogenous temporomandibular disorder
- disc / mobility-dominant TMJ disorder

## 1) Chronic pain associated with TMD
### Practice-changing sources
- BMJ rapid guideline for chronic pain associated with TMD (2023)
- Japanese Society for the Temporomandibular Joint primary-treatment guideline (2025 publication of 2023 edition)
- 2024 RCT of physiotherapy added to occlusal splint for myogenic TMD

### Main takeaways
- chronic painful TMD should be approached with conservative, reversible care first
- the BMJ guideline strongly supports CBT-based care, therapist-assisted mobilisation, manual trigger point therapy, supervised postural exercise, supervised jaw exercise and stretching, and usual care / education
- the same guideline recommends against many lower-value or more invasive options in routine chronic pain management, including irreversible oral splints, discectomy, and opioid-containing NSAID combinations
- the Japanese primary-treatment guideline supports self-administered mouth-opening exercise and stabilization-type oral appliances as primary treatments, with low-level laser therapy only conditionally suggested
- in a 2024 myogenic TMD RCT, adding musculoskeletal physiotherapy to education and occlusal splinting improved pain and ROM versus splint + education alone

### Implication for this KB
Keep the TMJ cluster centered on education, self-management, supervised exercise, manual therapy as an adjunct, cervical/postural integration, and selective dental collaboration rather than appliance-only or procedure-heavy care.
