# Shoulder Cluster Evidence Update

## Scope of this update
This page tracks higher-value evidence for the current shoulder cluster:
- rotator cuff-related shoulder pain / RC tendinopathy
- adhesive capsulitis / frozen shoulder
- traumatic anterior shoulder instability

## 1) Rotator cuff-related shoulder pain / RC tendinopathy
### Practice-changing sources
- JOSPT clinical practice guideline (2025)
- JOSPT exercise FITT systematic review with meta-analysis (2024)
- terminology / diagnostic framing editorial supporting the RCRSP label (2025)

### Main takeaways
- exercise remains first-line treatment
- imaging should not automatically drive the diagnosis in straightforward cases
- assessment and rehab can include RC tendinopathy with or without calcification and partial-thickness tears
- exercise prescription details matter, but there is no single magic exercise
- motor-control style exercise may improve disability versus nonspecific exercise in some time frames

### Implication for this KB
Use “rotator cuff-related shoulder pain / RC tendinopathy” rather than narrower structural labels unless there is a compelling reason to be more specific.

## 2) Adhesive capsulitis / frozen shoulder
### Practice-changing sources
- British Elbow and Shoulder Society pathway (2025)
- older APTA/JOSPT adhesive capsulitis CPG remains foundational
- NICE context acknowledged by BESS pathway

### Main takeaways
- frozen shoulder is a clinical diagnosis
- the course is often prolonged but generally self-limiting
- treatment goals focus on faster pain and function improvement rather than “curing” tissue pathology
- shared decision-making is central
- current pathway addresses physiotherapy, steroid injections, hydrodilatation, MUA, and arthroscopic capsular release

### Implication for this KB
Keep frozen shoulder as a separate stiffness-dominant pathway rather than folding it into generic shoulder pain.

## 3) Traumatic anterior shoulder instability
### Practice-changing sources
- international consensus statement on diagnosis, nonoperative management, and Bankart repair (2022)
- rehab / return-to-sport review (2024)
- ESSKA-ESA formal consensus part 1 on history, physical exam, and imaging (2026)
- BESS-commissioned postop stabilization rehab synthesis (2026)

### Main takeaways
- age, recurrence history, sport, and bone loss matter
- first-time and recurrent instability should not be treated as one undifferentiated group
- criteria-based RTS is preferable to time-only progression
- associated injuries become more relevant with age
- postop rehab guidance is becoming more structured, with domain-based recommendations

### Implication for this KB
Frame instability around recurrence risk, sport demands, and structural context rather than using a one-size-fits-all protocol.

## 4) Higher-level synthesis across shoulder guidelines
A 2024 systematic review of shoulder CPGs included guidelines on RC tendinopathy/tear, adhesive capsulitis, GH instability, GH osteoarthritis, calcific tendinitis, and AC disorders. This supports the modular design of this KB, where each disorder gets its own page rather than being merged into one “shoulder pain” note.
