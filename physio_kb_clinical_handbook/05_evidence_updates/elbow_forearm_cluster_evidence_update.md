# Elbow / Forearm Cluster Evidence Update

## Scope of this update
This page tracks higher-value evidence for the current elbow / forearm cluster:
- lateral elbow tendinopathy
- medial elbow tendinopathy / flexor-pronator overload

## 1) Lateral elbow tendinopathy
### Practice-changing sources
- 2024 systematic review and meta-analysis of non-invasive therapies
- 2024 systematic review comparing physical therapy versus corticosteroid injection
- 2021/2022 diagnostic accuracy systematic review of examination tests
- 2024 network meta-analysis for pain outcomes

### Main takeaways
- most non-invasive therapies still have low or very low certainty evidence
- there is no convincing single “best” passive modality
- clinical reasoning should prioritize load management, education, and progressive exercise
- corticosteroid injection may help short-term, but physical therapy appears more useful for intermediate / long-term outcomes with lower recurrence concerns
- painful gripping and resisted loading remain clinically useful, but no single special test should be overvalued

### Implication for this KB
Use a function-first tendon-load approach and treat braces, taping, electrotherapy, or manual therapy as adjuncts rather than the whole program.

## 2) Medial elbow tendinopathy
### Practice-changing sources
- evidence base is thinner than for lateral elbow pain
- clinical management remains largely extrapolated from tendon-load principles plus differential screening

### Main takeaways
- loading strategy and exposure modification still dominate practice
- neural and valgus-stress differentials matter more than they often do laterally

### Implication for this KB
Keep the medial page clinically pragmatic and differential-heavy rather than pretending there is a mature guideline base.
