## Practical takeaways that changed or were reinforced
### Lateral ankle sprain
- Use Ottawa-style fracture screening when indicated.
- Early external support and progressive weight-bearing are core acute management principles.
- Manual therapy can be used as an adjunct to improve pain-free mobility and gait.
- Rehabilitation should progress into functional exercise, balance, and sport-activity training rather than stopping once swelling settles.

### Chronic ankle instability
- Persistent symptoms after sprain are common enough that CAI should be actively screened for.
- Dynamic balance, perceived instability, dorsiflexion, and function should be measured, not assumed.
- Balance and strength training both help; combined programs appear particularly useful for patient-reported outcomes, while balance-focused work is strongly supported for dynamic balance.

### Midportion Achilles tendinopathy
- Progressive mechanical loading remains the central intervention.
- A heavy slow resistance approach or eccentric-based program can both be used.
- Patients should usually stay active within pain tolerance rather than fully resting.
- Stretching is optional when dorsiflexion limitation is relevant.
- Night splints are not recommended routinely; orthoses / heel lifts have mixed or insufficient evidence.
