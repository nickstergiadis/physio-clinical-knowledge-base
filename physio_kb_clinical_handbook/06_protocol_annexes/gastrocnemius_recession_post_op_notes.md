# Gastrocnemius Recession / Tendon Lengthening Post-Op Notes

## Why this is in an annex
This is a useful case-driven topic for practice, but the evidence base is much more protocol- and surgeon-specific than the main outpatient condition clusters. Use this note as a clinician reminder, not a universal protocol.

## Common rehab themes
- confirm exact procedure and WB restrictions
- manage swelling and incision tolerance
- restore gait progressively
- rebuild plantarflexor capacity and calf endurance
- address proximal contributors: gluteal strength, trunk control, single-leg balance
- monitor response to loading and footwear transitions

## Practical checkpoints
- boot / brace timeline
- dorsiflexion expectations and limits
- calf raise progression
- single-leg stance and gait symmetry
- swelling response to exercise
- return-to-walk / stairs / gym exposure

## Clinical caution
Surgeon instructions take priority. Tendon work, bony procedures, and combined foot / ankle operations can change timelines substantially.
