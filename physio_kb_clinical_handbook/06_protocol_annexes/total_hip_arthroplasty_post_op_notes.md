# Total Hip Arthroplasty Post-Op Notes

## Use this annex for
- elective THA rehab reference
- quick outpatient framing after surgeon / hospital discharge instructions are known

## Non-negotiable note
Always check:
- surgical approach if relevant locally
- dislocation precautions, if any
- weight-bearing status
- DVT / wound issues and medical complications
- assistive device progression guidance
- surgeon-specific restrictions for twisting, impact, and return to sport/work

## Practical rehab frame
### Early recovery phase
- wound / red-flag monitoring
- gait training and safe transfers
- progressive walking exposure
- pain and swelling management
- basic hip and lower-limb activation
- restore extension and functional ROM within precautions

### Function restoration phase
- wean assistive devices when gait quality allows
- progressive sit-to-stand, step, and stair work
- hip abductor / extensor strengthening
- balance and single-leg control progression as tolerated

### Capacity phase
- progressive resistance training
- normalized walking speed and endurance
- return to household, community, and gym function
- targeted higher-level balance and confidence work

## Practical priorities
- prioritize gait quality over rushing device removal
- address abductor weakness and lateral trunk shift
- build walking tolerance steadily
- progress load, not just ROM drills

## Evidence update note
Systematic review evidence suggests no single rehab program clearly outperforms all others after THA; diverse progressive programs appear broadly comparable. That makes good dosing, gait work, strength progression, and patient-specific goals more important than rigid allegiance to one branded protocol.
