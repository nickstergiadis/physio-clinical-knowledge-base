# Achilles Tendon Repair Post-Op Notes

## Use this annex for
- outpatient rehab planning after operative Achilles rupture repair

## Non-negotiable note
Always reconcile against:
- surgeon boot / wedge plan
- tendon quality and repair details
- wound healing
- DVT risk and medical factors

## Practical rehab frame
### Protection phase
- boot / wedge protection as prescribed
- respect plantarflexed protection position
- progressive weight-bearing only as allowed
- maintain proximal strength and general conditioning

### Early functional phase
- gradual reduction in wedges / boot dependence
- progressive ROM within allowed limits
- begin controlled calf activation when cleared
- gait normalization as loading tolerance improves

### Strength phase
- calf strengthening progression
- heel-raise pathway: double-leg to single-leg
- balance, hopping, and elastic loading introduced gradually

### Return phase
- running, jumping, cutting, and sport only after clear strength and functional milestones
- criterion-based return preferred

## Evidence update note
A 2022 meta-analysis reported favorable results with early weight-bearing plus mobilization after surgical repair, with quicker return to work / sport and no signal of worse major outcomes overall. A 2024 review suggested truly immediate mobilization may increase rerupture risk, so “early functional” should not mean reckless.
