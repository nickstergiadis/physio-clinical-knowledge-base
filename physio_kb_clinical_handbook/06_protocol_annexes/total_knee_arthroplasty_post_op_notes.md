# Total Knee Arthroplasty Post-Op Notes

## Use this annex for
- quick outpatient framing after primary TKA
- building practical plans when surgeon protocol is broad

## Non-negotiable note
Always reconcile against:
- surgeon precautions
- wound status / infection concern
- DVT / medical risk profile
- manipulation-under-anaesthesia history or arthrofibrosis risk

## Practical rehab frame
### Early phase
- swelling, pain, wound, DVT screening
- gait aid optimization
- ROM emphasis, especially extension
- quadriceps activation and transfer independence
- progressive walking exposure

### Strength / function phase
- progressive quadriceps and hip strengthening
- sit-to-stand, step, stair, balance, gait endurance
- continue ROM work where needed

### Advanced function phase
- higher-level strength and endurance
- community mobility, stairs, floor transfers where relevant
- realistic task goals rather than “normal knee” expectations

## Evidence update note
Systematic review evidence suggests many rehab programs after TKA lead to comparable pain, ROM, and ADL outcomes; acute-phase rehab may improve early strength, but postacute programs often converge.
