# Rotator Cuff Repair Post-Op Notes

## Use this annex for
- arthroscopic rotator cuff repair rehab planning
- quick protocol framing before reconciling with surgeon restrictions

## Non-negotiable note
Always check:
- tear size and tissue quality
- repair type
- concomitant biceps / labrum / subscap / acromioplasty procedures
- surgeon ROM and sling restrictions

## Practical rehab frame
### Early protection phase
- protect repair
- sling / immobilization per surgeon
- distal AROM, pain and swelling control
- protected passive or assisted shoulder motion only when allowed

### Motion restoration phase
- gradual ROM progression
- avoid provoking aggressive cuff load too early
- restore scapular control and low-load active movement when cleared

### Strength phase
- begin cuff and scapular strengthening once motion and tissue tolerance allow
- progress from isometrics to low-load isotonic work
- avoid heavy overhead loading too early

### Return-to-function phase
- restore overhead strength / endurance
- task or sport-specific progression
- criteria-based return preferred over time alone

## Evidence update note
Recent meta-analysis suggests early exercise after arthroscopic cuff repair may improve ROM and reduce postoperative stiffness risk, without clear pain/function superiority, but surgeon and tear-specific context still matters.
