# Gluteus Medius / Minimus Repair Post-Op Notes

## Use this annex for
- post-op lateral hip rehab after gluteal tendon repair
- quick planning before reconciling with the surgeon protocol

## Non-negotiable note
Always check:
- repair type and tissue quality
- abduction restrictions
- brace / WB restrictions
- whether bursectomy or additional procedures were performed
- surgeon timeline for abduction strengthening, single-leg work, and gait progression

## Practical rehab frame
### Protection phase
- respect WB and abduction restrictions
- protect repair from excessive tensile and compressive load
- gait with device as prescribed
- pain and swelling control
- maintain distal and trunk capacity within restrictions

### Motion and gait phase
- gradual restoration of hip ROM within allowed ranges
- normalize gait as loading restrictions reduce
- restore pelvic control without overloading the repair

### Strength phase
- delayed / progressive abductor loading per tissue and surgeon rules
- start with low-irritability control work
- progress to functional closed-chain and single-leg work when permitted

### Return-to-function phase
- build walking endurance
- stair, hill, and single-leg tolerance
- later return to gym and recreational demands

## Common pitfalls
- early aggressive abduction strengthening
- uncontrolled Trendelenburg loading too soon
- under-recognizing compressive irritation at the greater trochanter

## Evidence update note
Published outcome series suggest gluteal tendon repair can improve pain and patient-reported outcomes, but postop protocols remain procedure-specific and surgeon dependent. Protect the repair first, then build gait and abductor capacity progressively.
