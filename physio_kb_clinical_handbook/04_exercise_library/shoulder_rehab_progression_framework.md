# Shoulder Rehab Progression Framework

## Goal
Provide a reusable progression framework for the main non-surgical shoulder cluster in this KB:
- rotator cuff-related shoulder pain
- adhesive capsulitis / frozen shoulder
- traumatic anterior shoulder instability

## General principles
- match loading to irritability and dominant presentation
- separate pain-dominant, stiffness-dominant, and instability-dominant problems
- use task-specific progression, not just isolated band work
- keep rehab criteria-based and function-linked
- patient education and expectations influence adherence and outcomes

## Phase 1 — Calm + restore access
Use when:
- symptoms are highly irritable
- movement confidence is low
- acute post-injury or pain-dominant shoulder is limiting basic function

Targets:
- symptom calming
- protected motion access
- preserve confidence and everyday use
- establish patient understanding

Examples:
- pain-tolerable active-assisted or supported ROM
- submax cuff/scapular isometrics
- thoracic mobility / posture unloading where helpful
- gentle closed-chain or supported loading
- education on pacing and symptom response

## Phase 2 — Restore motion + baseline strength
Use when:
- symptoms are more predictable
- baseline daily use is improving

Targets:
- restore meaningful ROM
- build cuff and scapular capacity
- improve task tolerance

Examples:
- progressive active ROM and end-range access where appropriate
- cuff loading in tolerable planes
- scapular and trunk contribution work
- row / carry / supported press or elevation progressions
- mobility emphasis for frozen shoulder
- early dynamic stabilization for instability

## Phase 3 — Progressive capacity
Use when:
- recovery after loading is acceptable
- the patient is ready for more functional strength

Targets:
- heavier loading
- outer-range control
- endurance
- occupational / recreational rebuilding

Examples:
- heavier cuff and shoulder girdle loading
- landmine / dumbbell / cable progressions as appropriate
- overhead exposure
- deceleration and perturbation work
- instability-specific outer-range control
- frozen-shoulder function rebuilding in regained ROM

## Phase 4 — Return to performance / resilience
Use when:
- baseline function is restored enough for higher demand

Targets:
- sport / work-specific capacity
- speed and power where relevant
- confidence and recurrence prevention

Examples:
- overhead endurance circuits
- plyometric upper-limb loading
- throwing or contact preparation
- return-to-gym or return-to-work planning
- maintenance plan for symptom flares or recurrence risk

## Condition-specific notes
### Rotator cuff-related shoulder pain
- education + exercise are core treatment
- load modification should be strategic and temporary
- manual therapy can support but should not replace loading progressions

### Adhesive capsulitis
- stage and irritability matter
- avoid turning rehab into an aggressive stretching contest
- aim for function, pain control, and staged ROM restoration

### Traumatic anterior instability
- build outer-range control gradually
- include confidence / apprehension exposure and criteria-based RTS decisions
- recurrence risk and structural context may alter how conservative care is framed
