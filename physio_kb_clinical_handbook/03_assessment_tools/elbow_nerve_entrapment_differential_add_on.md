# Elbow Nerve / Entrapment Differential Add-On

## Use this add-on when
- lateral or medial elbow pain is not behaving like a straightforward tendinopathy
- paresthesia, weakness, clumsiness, or night symptoms are present
- local treatment has failed and the pattern feels neural or mixed

## Fast screen questions
- Any numbness / tingling? Where exactly?
- Any night symptoms or waking with the arm bent?
- Any grip loss, finger weakness, or dropping objects?
- Does elbow flexion, leaning on the elbow, or prolonged phone use provoke symptoms?
- Does repetitive supination / pronation or gripping provoke symptoms?
- Any neck symptoms, scapular/arm referral, or prior nerve history?

## Quick differential buckets
### Cubital tunnel syndrome
Think of this when:
- ring / little finger sensory symptoms are present
- symptoms worsen with sustained elbow flexion or external pressure
- dexterity / intrinsic hand endurance changes appear

### Radial tunnel syndrome
Think of this when:
- lateral proximal forearm pain is more distal than the classic lateral epicondyle spot
- pain is aggravated by supination, gripping, or repetitive forearm rotation
- sensory loss is absent or minimal

### Posterior interosseous nerve syndrome
Think of this when:
- there is finger / thumb extension weakness or clear motor change
- pain is not the whole story

### Cervical / proximal neural source
Think of this when:
- symptoms spread beyond the local elbow region
- neck movement or postures influence symptoms
- the local elbow exam is not convincing

## Objective add-on ideas
- sensory map
- grip and pinch comparison
- hand intrinsic and extensor motor screen
- symptom response to elbow flexion / local compression where relevant
- cervical screen
- upper-quarter neurodynamic or peripheral nerve provocation only if clinically appropriate and tolerated

## Escalation features
- progressive weakness
- intrinsic wasting
- persistent sensory loss
- marked dexterity loss
- atypical widespread neurology
- suspicion of systemic neuropathy or mass lesion

## Documentation shortcut
Document clearly whether the presentation is:
- tendon-dominant
- nerve-dominant
- mixed tendon + nerve
- referred / cervical-dominant
