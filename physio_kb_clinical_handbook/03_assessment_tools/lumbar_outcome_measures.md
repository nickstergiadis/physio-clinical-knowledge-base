# Lumbar Outcome Measures

## Primary options
### Oswestry Disability Index (ODI)
Use when:
- lumbar disability is moderate to high
- a spine-specific disability score is useful

Pros:
- well established
- widely used in spine rehab and research

### Roland-Morris Disability Questionnaire (RMDQ)
Use when:
- disability is milder to moderate
- you want a simple low back pain function scale

Pros:
- practical
- responsive in many non-specific LBP cases

### Patient-Specific Functional Scale (PSFS)
Use when:
- you want goals tied directly to the patient’s own tasks
- work, gym, parenting, driving, or sport tasks matter most

Pros:
- highly clinically relevant
- great for individualized progression

## Useful adjuncts
### Numeric Pain Rating Scale (NPRS)
- useful for irritability and change tracking
- should not be used alone to judge progress

### STarT Back Screening Tool
- useful at first contact / new episode to inform stratified care approach
- not an outcome measure for progress, but a prognostic / management aid

### Fear / confidence / expectation prompts
Use informally or with formal tools where needed if:
- high avoidance
- recovery stalls
- distress is clearly shaping disability

## Practical recommendation for this KB
Default lumbar tracking set:
- NPRS
- PSFS
- ODI or RMDQ
- STarT Back at initial episode triage when relevant
